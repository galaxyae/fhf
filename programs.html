Empowering Young Women & Girls

For Her Foundation Programs & Impact

While open to supporting projects in different communities, FHF primarily focuses its efforts on communities in which C-Quest Capital (CQC) has well-established carbon transformation programs.

FHF programs help women save time and energy by using fuel-efficient stoves. This also helps build stronger connections and trust within communities. These connections give FHF a better understanding of what women need and help make projects more successful.

The programs supported by For Women, help women and their families to take full advantage of these opportunities and provide a crucial support towards greater prosperity and resilience.
We know how awesome it is to have more programs that help people in our community. For Her Foundation is going to keep making sure that girls and women have lots of chances to get the help and opportunities they need.

By working with successful local and international partners, from NGOs to hospitals, For Women’s programs currently support

Helping moms and babies stay healthy by building a new maternity clinic and working with programs that help with fistulas. Having clean clinics means fewer germs for babies and enough space for pregnant moms to have their babies safely.
The health and well-being of mothers and babies are of paramount importance in any society. Maternal and child health not only impacts individual families but also shapes the overall health of a community. With a focus on enhancing care and support for expectant mothers and newborns, the establishment of a new maternity clinic signifies a significant step towards promoting better health outcomes.
By helping to build boreholes in places where there's not enough water, we're making sure that everyone in the community has access to clean water. This means that women and girls won't have to walk long distances just to get water anymore. And when there's a drought, we can make sure that there's still enough water for everyone to use. Access to clean water is essential for the health and well-being of communities worldwide. Water scarcity not only affects basic hygiene and sanitation but also has economic implications. Lack of clean water can lead to waterborne diseases, impacting productivity and healthcare costs.
Access to education for girls from Secondary School to University by providing scholarships and other essential needs, such as school uniforms, books, cash stipends, blankets and sanitary products.
Access to education is a fundamental right that empowers individuals and drives societal progress. However, for girls transitioning from secondary school to university, numerous challenges and barriers can impede their educational journey. From sociocultural norms and economic constraints to gender stereotypes and limited opportunities, girls often face unique obstacles in accessing quality education.
Enhanced food security for women and their families through providing drip irrigation kits and training that allow for food to be grown throughout the dry season.
Women play a crucial role in ensuring the well-being and nutrition of their families, making their access to sufficient and nutritious food a vital component of overall food security. However, women often face unique challenges that can hinder their ability to provide adequate meals for themselves and their loved ones.
Improved opportunities for women to expand their livelihoods through supporting programs that train women to master new skills and start new businesses (i.e., creating sanitary pads and liquid soaps) that inspire entrepreneurship and economic empowerment.
Women's livelihood support programs play a crucial role in advancing gender equality and empowering women to achieve economic independence. By providing women with access to skills training, financial resources, and market opportunities, these programs have the potential to transform lives and communities.
Contribute to girls’ empowerment by supporting programs focused on increasing self-esteem and changing perspectives about gender roles. School clubs, for instance, give a space for girls to share and process their lived experiences, and have been instrumental in igniting a sense of empowerment among girls from varied backgrounds.
Girls' empowerment is not just a matter of gender equality; it is a fundamental human right and a crucial driver of sustainable development. Despite progress in recent years, girls around the world continue to face significant challenges that hinder their growth and potential.
