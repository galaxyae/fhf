We are For Her Foundation

Eliminating obstacles so that all girls can reach new heights, and all women can achieve their dreams.

The For Her Foundation (FHF) is a non-profit organization based in Kampala, Uganda engaged in programming and country-wide policy work. FHF's goal is to make sure that all girls and women are treated fairly and have the same opportunities. We want to make sure that this equality lasts for a long time, not just for now. In pursuit of this mission, FHF supports policies and programmatic interventions that will lead to the self actualization, financial stability, personal success, and health and wellbeing of all self identified women in the region and beyond.

We seek equity for all self-identified women without regard to economic status, class, race, sexual orientation, gender identity, religion, sex, national origin, age, marital status, disability, or genetics

Empowering young people is central to our mission. We engage young self identified women as activists, donors, and leaders.

There was a sigh of relief on Friday 8th October as city Lawyer Shiela Namahe partnered up with singer Sheebah Karungi to launch ‘FOR HER Foundation’ a new menstrual health campaign aimed at raising awareness around the heightened menstruation related challenges women and girls in communities.

The campaign started off with the Founders Sheila Namahe and Sheebah Karungi ( For her foundation and Sheebah Cares respectively ) handing out 4000 packets of pads to the areas of Gaba ( Kitogo ) Namuwongo , Makindye and Nabisalu. The launch was preceded with a sensitization drive on menstrual hygiene management for the girls and women in the areas visited.

According to Sheila Namahe who is also a managing partner of Maxim advocates revealed that young girls engage in transactional sex to be able to afford pads and in the process are exposed to dangers of early pregnancy and HIV.

” If Condoms are Free why can’t pads be free so that every girl accesses them”,she said. A study by the duo also revealed that many girl in indigent communities abscond from school when in their periods due to lack of pads . They called out to government to join hands with civil society to right this wrong .
